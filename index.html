More about my background.

I got a degree in Physics from Universidad de las Américas Puebla, and a PhD in Physics (Computational Material Science) from The National Autonomous University of Mexico .

As a postdoctoral researcher I worked at the Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich, Juelich Forschungszentrum, and Imperial College London, where I preformed and analised quantum mechanis simulations to get a better understanding between the atomic structure of materials and the phenomena they display, which can lead to significant technological applications.

In my academic career, I have collaborated with researchers in Germany, USA, and Mexico. Successfully submitted a research collaboration proposal between Germany and UK, resulting in awarded grant for travelling during 2014-2015. Besides, I forged and nurtured a new network between German and Mexican research institutions, providing access to knowledge, expertise, and scientific facilities to Mexican PhD students in cutting-edge labs.
